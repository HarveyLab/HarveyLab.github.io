Bio

Jim graduated from Harvard University with an A.B. cum laude in neurobiology. As an undergraduate, Jim worked in Florian Engert's lab on the effects of neuromodulators on feeding behavior in zebrafish. He then worked as a research technician in Eugenia Chiappe's lab in Champalimaud on the visual behaviors that rely on small-object-detecting neurons in Drosophila. In graduate school, Jim has continued his interest in quantifying animal behaviors by developing computer vision techniques for behavior analysis in mice.
