Bio

Roberto graduated from Boston University with bachelor's degrees in neuroscience and economics. As an undergraduate at BU, Roberto worked in Alberto Cruz-Martin’s lab where he investigated cortico-thalamic networks and their relevance in visual cognition. As a summer researcher in David Cox’s laboratory, he investigated visual processing in rat primary visual cortex. As a research technician, Roberto has been working on an optogenetic project to understand neocortical involvement in spatial navigation decision-making tasks.
